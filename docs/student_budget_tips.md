# Student Budget Tips: Saving Money in Singapore

## Monthly Budget Overview

The average international student in Singapore spends SGD 1,200-2,500 per month. Here's a typical breakdown:

| Category | Budget Range | Percentage |
|----------|-------------|------------|
| Rent | $500-1,500 | 40-55% |
| Food | $300-600 | 20-25% |
| Transport | $80-150 | 5-10% |
| Utilities | $50-100 | 3-5% |
| Phone/Internet | $20-50 | 1-3% |
| Personal/Entertainment | $100-300 | 5-15% |

**Golden Rule:** Keep housing costs below 30-40% of your total monthly budget.

## Food: The Biggest Savings Opportunity

### Eat at Hawker Centres

Hawker centres are Singapore's secret weapon for cheap, delicious food. A full meal costs just $3-5.

**Best Budget Hawker Centres Near Universities:**
- NUS: Ayer Rajah Food Centre, Clementi Hawker Centre
- NTU: Pioneer MRT area food courts, Jurong West hawker centres
- SMU: Amoy Street Food Centre, Maxwell Food Centre, Tekka Centre
- General: Any HDB town will have a hawker centre within walking distance

**Typical Hawker Prices:**
- Chicken rice: $3.50-4.50
- Nasi lemak: $3-4
- Mixed vegetable rice (cai fan): $3-4.50
- Noodle soup (ban mian, fish soup): $4-5
- Roti prata: $1.50-3

### Cook at Home

Cooking 3-4 times a week can save you 40% on food costs.

**Where to Buy Groceries:**
- NTUC FairPrice: Most common supermarket, student-friendly prices
- Sheng Siong: Known for the lowest prices
- Giant: Good for bulk buying
- Wet Markets: Cheapest for fresh vegetables, meat, and fish

**Grocery Budget Tips:**
- Shop after 7 PM for discounted items nearing expiry
- Buy rice in 5-10 kg bags (much cheaper per serving)
- Split grocery costs with roommates
- Plan weekly meals to avoid food waste

### Use Food Apps for Deals

- **Burpple Beyond:** 1-for-1 dining deals at restaurants ($69/year, pays for itself quickly)
- **Eatigo:** 10-50% off restaurant bookings during off-peak hours
- **GrabFood/foodpanda:** Look for first-user discounts and flash deals
- **Too Good To Go:** Buy surplus food from restaurants at 50%+ off

## Transport: Smart Commuting

### Get a Student Concession Card

Apply for an EZ-Link Student Concession Card through your university. It offers significantly discounted MRT and bus fares.

**Cost Comparison (per trip):**
- Adult fare: $0.92-2.20
- Student concession: $0.42-0.59
- Monthly savings: $50-100 compared to adult fares

### Monthly Pass vs Pay-Per-Ride

- **Monthly pass:** $128/month for unlimited MRT + bus (only worth it if you travel very frequently)
- **Pay-per-ride with student card:** Usually cheaper for most students (around $60-80/month)

### Free Transfer Rules

- Transfer between MRT and bus within 45 minutes — you only pay the combined distance fare, not two separate fares
- Up to 5 transfers allowed per trip

### Avoid Expensive Options

- Grab/taxi: $8-15 per short trip — use only in emergencies or late at night
- Peak hour surcharges: Avoid taxi rides during 6-9 AM and 6 PM-midnight
- Late-night surcharges: 50% extra for taxis after midnight

### Save More on Transport

- Walk or cycle for short distances (under 1 km)
- Use campus shuttle buses (NUS, NTU have free internal shuttles)
- Travel before 7:45 AM for off-peak MRT discounts
- Download CityMapper or Google Maps to find the cheapest routes

## Housing: Reducing Your Biggest Expense

### Choose Affordable Areas

**Most Affordable Areas for Students (HDB room):**
1. Jurong West / Boon Lay: $500-750/month
2. Woodlands / Sembawang: $500-700/month
3. Pasir Ris: $500-750/month
4. Bedok: $550-800/month
5. Clementi: $700-1,000/month

### Share to Save

- Sharing a 3-room HDB ($1,800-2,400) with 2-3 people: $600-800 each
- Much cheaper than renting individual rooms
- Split utilities and internet costs

### Negotiate Your Lease

- Longer lease (12 months) often gets you a lower monthly rate
- Ask for utilities to be included in rent
- Negotiate during low-demand months (January-March, July-September)

## Phone and Internet

### Mobile Plans for Students

**Budget Options:**
- Giga (by StarHub): from $8/month for basic data
- Circles.Life: $18-28/month with no contract
- CMLink: Popular among Chinese students, cheap international calls
- Singtel prepaid: flexible top-up options

**Tip:** Most universities provide free Wi-Fi on campus, so you mainly need mobile data for commuting.

### Home Internet

- If your landlord doesn't include internet, split the cost with roommates
- Basic fibre broadband: $30-40/month, split 2-3 ways = $10-15 each

## Student Discounts and Perks

### Always Carry Your Student ID

Many places offer student discounts, but you need to ask and show your card.

**Where to Get Discounts:**
- Cinemas: $8-9 instead of $13-15 (weekday discounts)
- Museums: Many are free or discounted for students
- Software: Microsoft Office 365 free, GitHub Student Pack, Adobe discounts
- Attractions: Student rates at Gardens by the Bay, ArtScience Museum, etc.
- Gyms: ActiveSG membership at $2/entry for public sports facilities

### Free and Cheap Activities

- Parks and nature reserves: All free (MacRitchie, Bukit Timah, East Coast Park)
- National Library: Free membership, access to books, newspapers, and study spaces
- Community events: Check OnePA for free workshops and activities
- University clubs: Join student organizations for social activities

## Part-Time Work

### Rules for Student Pass Holders

- Up to 16 hours per week during the academic semester
- Full-time work allowed during official vacation periods
- Must be enrolled at an approved institution
- No need for a separate work permit

### Common Student Jobs

- Campus jobs: teaching assistant, library assistant, research assistant ($8-15/hour)
- Tuition: Private tutoring pays well ($20-50/hour depending on subject)
- F&B: Restaurant/cafe work ($8-12/hour)
- Freelance: Translation, graphic design, content writing

## Quick Tips

1. Open a local bank account (DBS/OCBC) to avoid international transaction fees
2. Use PayNow for free person-to-person transfers
3. Buy second-hand textbooks from seniors or university book exchanges
4. Check if your university library has e-book access before buying
5. Join your university's student Facebook/Telegram groups for second-hand deals
6. Apply for bursaries and financial aid — many universities have emergency funds
