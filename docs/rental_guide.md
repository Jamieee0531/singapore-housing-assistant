# Complete Rental Guide for International Students in Singapore

## Before You Start Searching

### Documents You Need

**Essential:**
- Valid passport
- Student pass or IPA letter (In-Principle Approval)
- Acceptance letter from your university
- Proof of financial means (bank statement or scholarship letter)

**Helpful:**
- Reference letter from previous landlord (if available)
- Emergency contact in Singapore or home country
- Copy of university enrollment confirmation

### Setting Your Budget

**Calculate Total Housing Costs:**
1. Monthly rent
2. Utilities (water, electricity, gas): $50-100/month
3. Internet: $30-50/month (if not included)
4. Maintenance fees (condo only): Usually included in rent
5. Initial deposit: 1-2 months rent
6. Agent fee (if applicable): Usually half month's rent

**Budget Rule:**
Keep housing costs below 30% of your monthly budget.

## Timeline for House Hunting

### 3 Months Before Arrival

**What to do:**
- Join university housing Facebook groups
- Research areas near your campus
- Understand typical rental prices
- Connect with current students

**Not recommended:**
- Signing contracts remotely without viewing
- Paying deposits before arriving in Singapore

### 1-2 Months Before Arrival

**What to do:**
- Start actively searching on PropertyGuru, 99.co
- Save listings that interest you
- Prepare your introduction message for landlords
- Book temporary accommodation for first 2 weeks

**Temporary Stay Options:**
- University hostels (if available)
- Budget hotels ($50-80/night)
- Hostels ($20-40/night)
- Airbnb ($40-70/night)

### Upon Arrival (First 2 Weeks)

**What to do:**
- View 5-10 properties in person
- Test your commute during peak hours
- Check the actual room size and condition
- Meet landlords and existing tenants
- Compare options and negotiate

## Finding Properties

### Online Platforms

**PropertyGuru**
- Largest property portal in Singapore
- Filter by location, price, room type
- Contact agents or landlords directly
- Mobile app available

**99.co**
- User-friendly interface
- Good map view function
- Detailed property information
- Direct landlord listings

**University Facebook Groups**
- NUS: "NUS Housing/Roommate Finder"
- NTU: "NTU Housing and Roommates"
- SMU: "SMU Housing"
- More casual listings, often from other students

**Student Forums**
- HardwareZone Forums (Property section)
- Reddit r/Singapore
- University online forums

### Working with Property Agents

**Advantages:**
- Save time (they pre-screen properties)
- Help with contract negotiations
- Assist with administrative matters
- Free for tenants (landlord pays the commission)

**Disadvantages:**
- May push expensive properties
- Commission pressure
- Not all listings are exclusive

**Tips:**
- Work with 1-2 agents maximum
- Be clear about your budget
- Don't sign anything immediately
- Ask about their commission structure

## Viewing Properties

### What to Check

**The Room:**
- Actual size (measure if possible)
- Natural lighting
- Air conditioning condition
- Storage space (wardrobe, shelves)
- Door lock and window security
- Noise level

**Common Areas:**
- Kitchen cleanliness and equipment
- Bathroom condition and number
- Living room space
- Laundry facilities
- Overall maintenance

**Building & Surroundings:**
- Distance to MRT/bus stop
- Nearby amenities (supermarket, food options)
- Safety of the neighborhood
- Elevator condition (if high floor)

**Questions to Ask Landlord:**
1. What is included in the rent?
2. How are utilities calculated and split?
3. What is the house rule? (guests, cooking time, etc.)
4. Who are the current housemates?
5. Is there a minimum lease period?
6. What are the reasons previous tenants left?

## Understanding Rental Contracts

### Standard Terms

**Lease Duration:**
- Minimum: Usually 6 months for HDB, flexible for Condo
- Notice period: 1-2 months for early termination
- Renewal options: Discuss before signing

**Rental Payment:**
- Usually monthly, due on 1st of each month
- Payment method: Bank transfer or check
- Late payment penalty: Check contract

**Deposit:**
- Amount: 1-2 months rent
- Refund conditions: Full refund if no damages
- Timeline: Within 14 days after moving out
- Deductions: Only for damages or unpaid bills

**Utilities:**
- Who pays: Usually tenant
- How to split: Based on number of occupants or per room
- Cap amount: Some contracts set monthly limits

### Important Clauses to Understand

**Maintenance Responsibility:**
- Landlord: Major repairs (AC, plumbing, structural)
- Tenant: Minor issues, daily cleaning
- Furniture: Check condition before moving in

**Early Termination:**
- Notice period: Usually 1-2 months
- Penalty: May forfeit deposit or pay remaining months
- Replacement tenant: Some landlords allow this option

**Renewal Terms:**
- Rent increase: Usually 3-5% for renewal
- Negotiable: Discuss before initial contract ends

## Red Flags and Scams

### Common Scams

**Fake Listings:**
- Photos from other properties
- Prices too good to be true
- Landlord asking for payment before viewing

**Identity Theft:**
- Asking for passport or bank details upfront
- Requesting wire transfers to foreign accounts

**Bait and Switch:**
- Showing good room, offering different room
- Claiming advertised room "just rented"

### How to Protect Yourself

1. **Never pay before viewing** in person
2. **Verify landlord identity** (NRIC for Singaporeans, passport for foreigners)
3. **Get official receipts** for all payments
4. **Read contract thoroughly** before signing
5. **Take photos** of room condition on move-in day
6. **Keep all communications** in writing (WhatsApp, email)

## Moving In

### Checklist

**Before Move-In Day:**
- Take photos/videos of the room and common areas
- Note any existing damages
- Test all appliances
- Check internet speed
- Get keys and access cards

**On Move-In Day:**
- Do a walkthrough with landlord
- Sign condition report
- Get receipts for deposit and first rent
- Exchange contact numbers
- Get WiFi password and utility account details

**First Week:**
- Register your address (if required)
- Set up utility account (if in your name)
- Meet all housemates
- Understand house rules
- Familiarize with recycling and trash disposal

## Living with Housemates

### House Rules Discussion

**Essential Topics:**
- Quiet hours (usually 11pm-7am)
- Guest policies
- Kitchen usage and cleaning
- Bathroom schedules
- Common area cleaning rotation
- Temperature settings for AC
- Food sharing or separate groceries

### Conflict Resolution

**If issues arise:**
1. Talk directly with the person involved
2. Bring up concerns early and politely
3. Suggest practical solutions
4. Involve landlord only if necessary
5. Document serious issues in writing

## Moving Out

### 1 Month Before

- Give official notice in writing
- Discuss final inspection with landlord
- Start looking for new place (if relocating)
- Plan moving logistics

### 2 Weeks Before

- Deep clean your room and common areas
- Repair any minor damages
- Return borrowed items
- Cancel utilities (if in your name)

### Move-Out Day

- Final walkthrough with landlord
- Return all keys and access cards
- Take photos of clean room
- Provide forwarding address for deposit refund
- Get written confirmation of move-out

### Getting Deposit Back

**Timeline:**
- Usually within 14 days
- May take up to 30 days with utility bills

**Possible Deductions:**
- Cleaning fees (if left dirty)
- Repair costs (for damages)
- Unpaid utility bills
- Early termination penalties

**If Disputes Arise:**
- Refer to move-in photos
- Check contract terms
- Small Claims Tribunal (for amounts under $10,000)
- University housing office may provide advice
