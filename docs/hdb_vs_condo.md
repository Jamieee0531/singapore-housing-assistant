# HDB vs Condo: Complete Guide for International Students

## What is HDB?

HDB stands for Housing & Development Board. These are government-built public housing apartments that make up about 80% of Singapore's residential properties.

### HDB Characteristics

**Price Range:**
- Common room (shared bathroom): $600-900 per month
- Master room (private bathroom): $800-1,200 per month
- Whole unit (3-room): $1,800-2,500 per month

**Location:**
- Found in every residential area across Singapore
- Usually well-connected to MRT stations
- Near community facilities like hawker centers and wet markets

**Facilities:**
- Basic amenities: playgrounds, fitness corners, void decks
- Some newer HDB estates have better facilities
- Community spaces for residents

**Living Environment:**
- More local Singaporean neighbors
- Authentic Singapore living experience
- Family-oriented neighborhoods

## What is Condo?

Condominiums are private residential developments with premium facilities and services.

### Condo Characteristics

**Price Range:**
- Common room: $1,000-1,500 per month
- Master room: $1,500-2,200 per month
- Whole unit (2-bedroom): $2,800-4,500 per month

**Location:**
- Often closer to business districts and universities
- Near shopping malls and restaurants
- Better accessibility in central areas

**Facilities:**
- Swimming pool (almost all condos)
- Gym and fitness center
- Tennis/basketball courts
- BBQ pits
- Function rooms
- 24/7 security with access card system
- Sometimes include sauna, jacuzzi, or mini-mart

**Living Environment:**
- Mix of locals and expatriates
- More cosmopolitan atmosphere
- Younger demographic (many working professionals)

## Key Differences Summary

### For Budget-Conscious Students

**Choose HDB if:**
- You want to save $300-500 per month on rent
- You're comfortable with basic amenities
- You want to experience authentic local culture
- You don't mind sharing facilities with many residents

**Choose Condo if:**
- Premium facilities are important to you
- You value 24/7 security
- You prefer a swimming pool and gym
- You can afford the higher rent

### For Location Priority

**HDB Advantage:**
- More options in mature estates
- Generally near MRT stations
- Lower rental competition

**Condo Advantage:**
- Often walking distance to universities
- Closer to central business district
- Near international schools and expat areas

## Rental Process Differences

### HDB Rental

**Requirements:**
- Valid student pass
- Minimum 6-month lease
- Need landlord approval (HDB regulations)
- Utility bills usually separate

**Deposit:**
- Typically 1-2 months rent
- May require additional utility deposit

### Condo Rental

**Requirements:**
- Valid student pass
- Flexible lease terms (some allow 3 months)
- Professional property agents often involved
- Utilities may be included or separate

**Deposit:**
- Usually 2 months rent
- More standardized contracts

## Making Your Decision

### Budget Calculation Example

**HDB (Common Room):**
- Rent: $800/month
- Utilities: $50-80/month
- Internet: $20/month (split)
- **Total: $870-900/month**

**Condo (Common Room):**
- Rent: $1,200/month
- Utilities: Often included
- Internet: Often included
- **Total: $1,200-1,300/month**

**Monthly Difference: $300-400**

## Important Notes

### For First-Time Renters

1. **Visit before committing** - Never rent without viewing
2. **Check transport time** - Test your commute during peak hours
3. **Read the contract carefully** - Understand all terms
4. **Verify with existing tenants** - Ask about landlord and living conditions
5. **Budget for initial costs** - Deposit + 1st month + agent fee (if any)

### Red Flags to Avoid

- Landlord asking for full payment before viewing
- No proper contract or receipt
- Rooms much cheaper than market rate
- Landlord refuses to meet in person
- Property not matching online photos
